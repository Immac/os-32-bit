26 /*===========================================================================
27  *
28  * Fixed width types.
29  *
30  * When doing any kind of low level system development, one can't use
31  * the standard C data types because C data types are not the same size
32  * on all architectures. The following types are defined (differently)
33  * for all architectures to provide a common set of data types.
34  *
35  * From the "Linux Device Drivers" book (chapter 11) available at
36  * http://lwn.net/Kernel/LDD3/
37  *
38  * arch Size: char short int long ptr long-long u8 u16 u32 u64
39  * i386 1 2 4 4 4 8 1 2 4 8
40  * alpha 1 2 4 8 8 8 1 2 4 8
41  * armv4l 1 2 4 4 4 8 1 2 4 8
42  * ia64 1 2 4 8 8 8 1 2 4 8
43  * m68k 1 2 4 4 4 8 1 2 4 8
44  * mips 1 2 4 4 4 8 1 2 4 8
45  * ppc 1 2 4 4 4 8 1 2 4 8
46  * sparc 1 2 4 4 4 8 1 2 4 8
47  * sparc64 1 2 4 4 4 8 1 2 4 8
48  * x86_64 1 2 4 8 8 8 1 2 4 8
49  *
50  *===========================================================================*/
